Curriculum

Cybersecurity Education

What secondary school students will learn about staying safe online — and the hands-on activities they'll do.

What They Will Learn

Essential cybersecurity knowledge for the digital age

Topic 1

Understanding Cybersecurity

Learn what cybersecurity means and why it matters. Students explore how digital systems are protected and their role in staying safe online.

Topic 2

Recognizing Common Cyber Threats

Identify phishing, malware, scams, and other threats. Students learn to spot suspicious links, emails, and messages before they cause harm.

Topic 3

Safe Use of Devices and the Internet

Best practices for secure browsing, strong passwords, software updates, and protecting devices. Build habits that keep you safe every day.

Topic 4

Cyberbullying and Digital Citizenship

Understand cyberbullying, its impact, and how to respond. Learn what it means to be a responsible, respectful digital citizen.

Topic 5

Protecting Digital Identity

Safeguard personal information, privacy settings, and online reputation. Students learn what to share — and what to keep private.

What They Will Do

Hands-on activities that bring cybersecurity to life

Cyber Safety Review Sessions

Guided discussions and reviews that reinforce key safety concepts. Students reflect on real scenarios and practice making smart decisions online.

Interactive Group Mini-Projects

Collaborative projects where teams design security solutions, create awareness campaigns, or solve security puzzles together.

TeenTech CTF (Capture the Flag)

Complete challenges in our Capture the Flag competition. Students apply their skills to find hidden flags, crack codes, and solve security puzzles.
